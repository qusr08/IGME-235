Prichi

This is my final project I made for during my 2021 fall semester at the Rochester Institute of Technology for IGME-235. It was made over the course of a couple weeks in Javascript with PixiJS. I made it entirely by myself with virtually no outside assets/libraries used. I used sfxr to make the sound effects and paint.NET to make the art. I even made my own font for the game on Fontstruct.

The premise of the game is "dungeon chess", where you have to navigate your chess piece around a randomly-generated level to eliminate all of the other chess pieces. Your goal is to survive as many levels as possible!

I really enjoy the idea of this project, and can definitely see myself developing the game further into a more content-rich game. I have a few more ideas for where the game can go, but I don't think it could be a full release of any kind. I'm very happy with all the sounds, graphics, and polish that I managed to achieve for this game. I am a big fan of turn-based puzzle games, and wanted to make this final project a game that I would enjoy playing.

Blockchain

This game was made in 48 hours for the GameMaker's Toolkit Game Jam 2021! The theme for the jam was "Joined Together", and so this game is all about chaining various blocks together to solve puzzles. The game was made with Unity and C#, with art done in paint.NET. I did all of the programming, art, and helped with the level design. My friend Joe Ferrugio helped with the level design and made the music. Overall, we placed in the top 10% of submissions, out of over 6000 total.

In Blockchain, you move around your character block and connect to various other blocks with different properties. You can use these other blocks to press buttons, open doors, or get across gaps. Each color of block has their own rules about which blocks they can stick to, which leads to some very interesting puzzle ideas. When making this game, I wanted to make sure that it was able to be finished, since we only have 48 hours to complete it. It was the first game jam I have ever attempted, and didn't have very high expectations. We shot for top 50% at least, and hoped for the best. When we first got the theme, we settled on the idea of moving different blocks around because it was simple, easily expanded upon, and (somewhat) intuitive for the player to figure out.

Currently, I have no plans to continue the development of this project in its current state, but I think it stands pretty strongly as a game jam submission. However, I do have ideas on how to make the gameplay more interesting and engaging which I will hopefully turn into another project at a later date.

Wumpus

I created this project during my junior year of high school for a computer science class. I created it by myself and coded it entirely from scratch in Java. The assignment in the class was originally just a text-based adventure, but I turned that idea into an actual game with graphics. I used sfxr to make the sound effects and paint.NET to make the art. The (admittedly) poorly-made music was created in Bosca Ceoil and the font I made using Fontstruct.

In Wumpus, you play as a person trying to kill the mysterious wumpus creature. You can't see much of your surroundings, so it's hard to tell where the wumpus is in relation to you. You get cues to tell you if something is nearby, like a hole in the floor or the wumpus. There are various items that you can find, like a bow, sword, trap, or torch. Each item has a different function and allows you to better prepare yourself to face the wumpus. Your goal is to kill the wumpus using the bow or sword before the wumpus kills you!

Looking back at this project, I really enjoyed making it and I am really proud of the final result, even to this day. I remember everyone else in my class being amazed at what I was able to create, which was very motivating. After knowing what I know now, and looking back at the code I wrote, there are some glaring errors that I can't believe I made, but it really demonstrated to myself how far I've come as a programmer and as a game developer. I definitely won't try to develop this game further, but it is nice to have it saved so I can keep looking at it in the future.

Collab

Collab is a small game that I made in about 2 months to get myself more familiar with Unity and C#. I created it by myself with the exception of my friend Joe Ferrugio who made the music using Bosca Ceoil. I create the art in paint.NET, the sound effects using sfxr, and the font using Fontstruct.

The game is a simple 2 player puzzle-platforming game were each player can switch the ability of their character to reach the end of the level. The abilities are super jump, super speed, and shrinking to a smaller size. For each level, the players only have a combined amount of times they can switch their powers, so you have to be strategic about it. Both players need to reach the end of the level to beat it!

My friend Joe and I have discussed a few ideas about how to expand upon this game and make it a full release, but I am still unsure if it will happen. The end result definitely helped me improve my game development skills in Unity and C#, but I can't picture a full game come from this concept.
